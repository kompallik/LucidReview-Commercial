The Workforce Reality

Your best reviewer just retired.
The next one takes 6 months.

This is not a future risk. It is the operational reality at health plans today. And it compounds every year.

6 mo
Onboarding time for a new UM reviewer
A new nurse reviewer needs 6 months before they can work independently on complex cases. During that time, they require supervision, make more errors, and reduce the throughput of the reviewer training them. Every departure restarts this clock.
High
Turnover rate in utilization management roles
UM reviewers burn out on repetitive, documentation-heavy work. The irony: the cases that drain them most — chasing missing records, writing the same denial rationale for the 500th time — are exactly the cases AI handles best.
↑↑
Proportion of UM staff approaching retirement
The UM workforce skews toward experienced clinicians who are now within 5–10 years of retirement. The institutional knowledge they carry — clinical intuition, policy patterns, edge-case judgment — cannot simply be rehired. It must be systematized before it walks out.
82.7%
Cases arriving with documentation gaps
4 in 5 cases reach your reviewer missing something. They spend the first half of every review chasing records, calling providers, and waiting for fax responses. This is not clinical work. It is administrative overhead. AI eliminates it before the case ever reaches a human.
💡
Not layoffs. Liberation.
LucidReview handles the routine so your reviewers can focus on what only they can do: complex clinical judgment, member advocacy, and physician peer-to-peer reviews. The productivity gain doesn’t come from reducing headcount — it comes from radically increasing the value of every hour your clinical team works.
